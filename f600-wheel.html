F600 Steering Wheel — Telemetry + Controls

A custom Formula 600 steering wheel with an integrated display, buttons, and a compact motorsport-ready wiring package.

Skills learned:
Fusion 360 CAD DFM / fabrication planning OMAX waterjet 3D printing (ASA) Arduino / ESP32 Embedded wiring + integration

Overview

I designed and built a custom Formula 600 steering wheel that combines driver controls and real-time telemetry in a compact package. The goal was to create something that feels purpose-built for motorsport: rigid, clean, serviceable, and easy to read at speed.

The wheel was modeled in Fusion 360, then fabricated using a waterjet-cut metal plate for stiffness and durability. I printed ASA shrouds to protect electronics and wiring from heat and vibration while keeping the assembly durable and easy to disasemble for service.

Result: a rigid wheel with integrated display + controls, clean harness routing, and a layout that’s easy to upgrade as the car evolves.

What it does

Telemetry display: real-time readouts (e.g., speed, RPM, oil pressure) on an Arduino/ESP32-based system.

Driver controls: button layout designed for quick reach and easy differentiation while driving.

Motorsport packaging: compact wiring + sensor integration, designed to be serviceable and robust under vibration.

Build highlights

Mechanical: waterjet-cut metal wheel plate (OMAX) for stiffness + precision.

Enclosure: ASA 3D-printed shroud components for durability and heat resistance.

Electronics: Arduino/ESP32 code to drive the display and handle IO (buttons/sensors/telemetry).

Integration: compact wiring layout to reduce snag risk and keep troubleshooting simple.
