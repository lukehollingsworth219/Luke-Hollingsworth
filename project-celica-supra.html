Overview

This build is about making an older platform genuinely fast and reliable on track, not just modified. The goal is to run competitive lap times against newer MX-5s, BMWs, and Porsches, cars that are far newer costing several times more. This will be acheived through a focus on chassis setup, reliability, and cornering stability rather than chasing power alone.

The work combines performance upgrades (newer engine, coilovers, custom strut brace, adjustable sway bars, race brake pads) with restoration-driven fixes like bushing replacement and a differential rebuild to bring the car back to a predictable baseline. From there, each change is treated as an experiment: design, build, test, and iterate.

This Supra is both a learning platform and a testbed for ideas—whether that’s CAD-designed parts, 3D-printed prototypes, welded fabrication, or setup changes validated through seat time.

Result: improved stability at speed, more consistent turn-in, and better braking confidence.

Suspension Refresh — Checklist

What’s been touched so far for handling + consistency.

  • Coilovers: installed + baseline ride height -> lowered decreasing body roll, increased handling, corner balance, damping.
  • Camber plates: installed to reach alignment targets (CAMBER/toe/caster) increased camber for track performance.
  • Adjustable sway bar: installed + settings tested (soft/medium/stiff) with notes.
  • Strut brace: fabricated, fitted, clearances checked, torqued.
  • Bushings: replaced worn bushings (control arms / steering / rear diff mounts as applicable).
  • Hardware: re-torqued under load (ride-height torque), paint-marked critical fasteners.
  • Clearance checks: full lock + full compression clearance verified (tires, fenders, brake lines).
  • Bump steer: checked + steering response notes logged.
  • Track validation: tire wear/temps + driver feedback used for iteration.

Next test: adjust sway bar + front damping, re-check temps, update alignment if needed.

Engine Swap: 5M-GE → 7M-GE (1989 Cressida)

Goal: get a noticeable bump in usable power without turning the car into a fragile science project.

The original 5M-GE did its job, but it felt outgunned on straights and uphill pulls—especially when trying to hang with newer MX-5s, BMWs, and Porsches. The fix wasn’t “big power,” it was a smarter baseline: a later-generation 7M-GE from a 1989 Toyota Cressida for more torque, better parts availability, and easier long-term maintenance.

Power change

  • Before (5M-GE): 145 hp • 150 lb-ft
  • After (7M-GE): 190 hp • 200 lb-ft
  • Net gain: + 45 hp • + 50 lb-ft (biggest difference is midrange torque)

Performance outcome

  • 0–60 estimate: improved by ~ 4 seconds (traction/gearing/tire dependent)
  • Track feel: stronger pull out of corners + less “waiting” for speed on short straights
  • Usability: less stress at sustained pace because the engine isn’t always working as hard

Reliability + serviceability wins

  • Parts availability: broader support + easier sourcing for maintenance items
  • Better platform to build on: more room to grow (cooling, tuning, future upgrades)
  • Track-minded baseline: modernizable maintenance, less sketchy one-off fixes

Note: like any older Toyota inline-6, the swap still lives or dies by cooling, clean wiring, and staying on top of gaskets/hoses/fluids.

Bottom line: The 7M-GE isn’t about chasing a huge number—it’s about a stronger, more sustainable engine that makes the car quicker everywhere and easier to keep alive with real track use.

Track Alignment — Setup Notes

Alignment changes focused on consistency, tire life, and confidence at the limit.

Key alignment principles (track-focused)

  • Negative camber: increases tire contact patch under cornering load → better mid-corner grip.
  • Toe-out (front): improves turn-in response and steering feel.
  • Toe-in (rear): increases stability under braking and high-speed corner exit.
  • Caster: improves straight-line stability and steering self-centering.

Baseline setup (example track numbers)

  • Front camber: −2.5° to −3.0°
  • Rear camber: −1.5° to −2.0°
  • Front toe: 0 to slight toe-out (~0.05°)
  • Rear toe: slight toe-in (~0.10°)
  • Caster: max achievable within suspension geometry

Exact values tuned based on tire temps, track layout, and driver feel.

Validation process

  • Tire temperatures: inside/middle/outside readings after sessions
  • Wear pattern: even shoulder wear vs edge rollover
  • Driver feedback: turn-in confidence, mid-corner balance, exit stability
  • Iteration: one change at a time, logged between sessions

Result: predictable front-end grip, reduced understeer, and a setup that holds up across multiple sessions.
